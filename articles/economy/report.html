U.S. economy grew at 2% pace in first quarter

AP reported that the U.S. economy expanded at a 2% annual pace from January through March 2026. The report said growth recovered from last fall’s 43-day federal government shutdown, while the outlook remains affected by the Iran war.

Why it matters: GDP growth is a broad measure of economic momentum, and uncertainty around energy prices and conflict can affect business investment and consumer spending.

U.S. inflation gauge jumps as gas prices rise

AP reported that a key inflation measure watched by the Federal Reserve rose 0.7% in March from February. Compared with a year earlier, prices rose 3.5%, the biggest increase in almost three years, according to the Commerce Department data cited by AP.

Why it matters: Higher inflation can delay interest-rate cuts and raise costs for households and businesses.

Fed minutes show more openness to possible rate hikes

Reuters reported that Federal Reserve officials raised their 2026 inflation outlook because of a war-related oil shock. The minutes showed that some officials favored opening the door to interest-rate hikes.

Why it matters: Federal Reserve policy affects borrowing costs for mortgages, credit cards, business loans, and financial markets.

ECB policymaker warns euro zone recession risks are real

Reuters reported that Yannis Stournaras, governor of the Bank of Greece and a member of the European Central Bank’s Governing Council, said euro zone recession concerns are “real and justified.” He cited the Iran war, energy-price uncertainty, and weakening economic momentum.

Why it matters: Recession risks in the euro zone can influence central-bank decisions, trade, investment, and global market confidence.
