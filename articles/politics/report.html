Supreme Court weakens Voting Rights Act provision in Louisiana map case

The U.S. Supreme Court ruled 6-3 against a Louisiana congressional map that included a second majority-Black district. Reuters and AP reported that the ruling limits how race may be used in drawing voting districts.

Why it matters: The decision could reshape congressional redistricting fights before the 2026 midterm elections.

Louisiana suspends congressional primaries after Supreme Court ruling

AP reported that Louisiana suspended its congressional primaries after the Supreme Court invalidated the state’s map. The delay gives state officials time to pursue new district boundaries.

Why it matters: Election scheduling and district lines can affect voter representation and control of congressional seats.

Courts rebuff Trump administration push for state voter rolls

Reuters reported that Democratic-led states have won legal challenges against the Trump administration’s effort to obtain state voter-roll data. The disputes come as the November 2026 midterm elections approach.

Why it matters: The cases test the limits of federal authority over election administration, which is largely handled by states.

Canada’s Liberals gain majority under Prime Minister Mark Carney

Reuters reported that Canada’s Liberal government gained a parliamentary majority after an opposition legislator joined the party. Prime Minister Mark Carney said a majority would help his government address trade pressures and domestic priorities.

Why it matters: A majority gives the government more ability to pass legislation without relying on opposition support.
