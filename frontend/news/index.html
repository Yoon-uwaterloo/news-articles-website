The story right now

Where things stand

The biggest story across desks this cycle is the slow recalibration of the global economy. Growth in the major advanced economies is tracking close to trend but with thinning margins, while several emerging markets are picking up where the developed world is cooling. Inflation has eased toward central-bank targets in most of the OECD, though the path back to two percent has been bumpier than policy makers signalled a year ago. Markets are pricing modest rate cuts through the end of the year, and they have repriced that view at least three times so far.

On the policy side, election cycles in several large economies are reshaping fiscal priorities. Industrial policy — subsidies, export controls, and supply-chain conditioning — has moved from the margins to the centre of the conversation, both in capitals and on company earnings calls. Regulatory action around competition, AI, and labour rules is producing a steady flow of stories, and the courts are increasingly the venue where the actual ground rules get settled.

Technology coverage is dominated by the buildout phase of the AI cycle. Compute capacity, energy demand, and the underlying chip supply chain are running into one another in ways that touch utilities, real estate, and trade policy. At the same time, the more conventional software and consumer-tech beats have not gone quiet — platform regulation, payments, and the slow drip of antitrust enforcement all continue to generate headlines.

On the data side, CPI and PPI prints are converging again after several months of divergence, and the labour market is gradually loosening from extremely tight conditions without (so far) breaking. Interest-rate paths remain the single most important variable for both equity and credit valuations, and government spending — particularly on defence and on energy-transition projects — is the line item to watch over the coming quarters.
