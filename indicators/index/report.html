Consolidated Economic Indicator Summary (through early 2026)

CPI

Summary: Post‑2021 inflation peaked in mid‑2022 and has largely cooled but remains above 2%. Headline CPI and PCE have converged since the surge, while median CPI shows a more persistent underlying signal.

  • Peaks and latest: CPI YoY peak 8.98% (Jun 2022); PCE YoY peak 7.24% (Jun 2022). Latest (Mar 2026): CPI YoY 3.29%, PCE YoY 3.50%, median CPI monthly 0.19% (~2.28% annualized).
  • Recent momentum: 3‑month annualized CPI ≈ 5.33%, PCE ≈ 5.61%, highlighting some short‑run firming despite lower year‑over‑year readings.
  • Two‑year averages: CPI ≈ 2.79% (last 24 months) vs 8.00% in 2022; PCE ≈ 2.67% vs 6.54% in 2022.
  • Cross‑measure alignment: CPI and PCE YoY correlation ≈ 0.992; dispersion across gauges has narrowed.

Key caveats: sample begins in 2021 (post‑pandemic), one missing CPI observation (Oct 2025), and median CPI is reported as monthly percent change (not directly an index).

PPI

Summary: Upstream price pressure eased after mid‑2022 but reaccelerated from late‑2023 into 2024–26. The goods and commodity side—mining, manufacturing, transportation—has driven the pickup; services remain steadier.

  • Headline commodity index: All Commodities = 274.1 (Mar 2026). Peak 280.3 (Jun 2022); trough 249.9 (Dec 2023).
  • Recent changes: MoM +1.78% (Mar 2026); YoY +6.03%. The move from Dec 2023 trough to Mar 2026 implies a ~9.7% rebound.
  • Sector detail: Manufacturing 265.3 (Mar 2026; YoY +6.22%); Mining 245.6 (volatile; YoY +8.14%); Information and many services show smaller, steadier gains.
  • Interpretation: Reacceleration is concentrated in goods/commodity sectors; PPI series are not seasonally adjusted here, so month‑to‑month readings can reflect seasonal patterns.

GDP

Summary: Real activity continued to expand through early 2026 but with uneven quarter‑to‑quarter momentum. Services have been the steadier support; goods and inventory/trade swings caused episodic softness.

  • Headline growth: Real GDP (Q/Q, SAAR) = 2.0% (Q1 2026). Real GDP YoY ≈ 2.7%.
  • Real final sales (Q/Q, SAAR) = 1.6% (Q1 2026), indicating domestic demand growth somewhat below headline GDP.
  • Sample features: average quarterly growth ≈ 2.93%; notable negative quarters in sample include 2022 Q1 and 2025 Q1; recent pattern: 3.8% (2025 Q2), 4.4% (2025 Q3), 0.5% (2025 Q4), then 2.0% (2026 Q1).
  • Composition: consumption share ≈ 68.1% (2025); services share rose to ~47.0% (2025) while goods share fell to ~21.2%.
  • Correlations: GDP vs final sales (q/q) ≈ 0.73; GDP vs GDI q/q ≈ 0.63; goods are more cyclical (GDP goods QoQ corr ≈ 0.83 with GDP QoQ), services smoother.

Interest Rate

Summary: Policy and market rates tightened sharply in 2022–23, peaked in mid/late‑2023, and have eased since—but remain substantially above the low‑rate 2021 baseline. Real yields moved from deeply negative in 2021 to solidly positive by late 2023.

  • Key levels (latest 30 Apr 2026 unless noted): EFFR = 3.64% (peak 5.33% on Jul 27, 2023); Primary Credit Rate = 3.75%; 3‑month T‑bill = 3.59%; 1‑year T‑bill = 3.57%.
  • Policy uncertainty: KCPRU = 1.195 (sample avg ≈ 1.15; peak 2.18 on Mar 15, 2023).
  • Real yields: 5‑yr TIPS = 1.35%; 10‑yr TIPS = 1.94% (large rise from 2021 averages when real yields were negative).
  • Interpretation: rates have eased from peak but remain ~3.4–3.6 percentage points above 2021 averages; spreads and term structure show markets priced elevated tightening through 2022–23 and partial normalization since.

Unemployment Rate

Summary: The labor market has moved from exceptionally tight conditions in 2022–23 toward normalization. The seasonally adjusted unemployment rate is near FOMC longer‑run estimates but has risen gradually rather than abruptly.

  • Latest: Unemployment rate (SA) = 4.3% (Mar 2026). Non‑seasonally adjusted = 4.3% (Mar 2026).
  • Sample stats: mean ≈ 4.19%, median ≈ 4.05%, sample min 3.4% (Apr 2023), sample max 6.4% (Jan 2021).
  • Trend: rate fell from 6.4% (Jan 2021) to 3.4% (Apr 2023), then drifted up to 4.3% by Mar 2026—about +0.9 percentage point above the trough and roughly in line with the FOMC longer‑run midpoint (~4.15%).
  • Movement style: increases have been gradual (average monthly change ≈ -0.034 percentage point in full sample; latest month change ≈ -0.1 ppt), suggesting normalization rather than abrupt weakening.

Overall Synthesis

Across indicators through early 2026: inflation gauges (CPI/PCE/median) show meaningful disinflation from 2022 peaks but remain above target with some recent firming; PPI signals renewed upstream pressure driven by goods and commodities; GDP data show continued expansion but more uneven quarterly momentum with services the stabilizing force; interest rates and real yields have moved down from 2023 peaks but are still materially tighter than the 2021 baseline; and the unemployment rate has risen modestly toward FOMC longer‑run estimates, consistent with gradual labor‑market rebalancing rather than a sudden downturn.

Implication for policy and forecasting: the snapshot points to a still‑restrictive financial environment and to inflation and upstream price dynamics that warrant continued monitoring. The economy remains in expansion but is more vulnerable to goods‑side shocks and quarter‑to‑quarter swings; labor‑market slack is rising slowly, limiting but not eliminating inflationary pressure.
